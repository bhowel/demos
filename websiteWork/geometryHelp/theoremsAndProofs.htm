Theorems and Proofs

Overview

In this section, you will learn how to prove an example theorem. Before starting, you should know the following terms.

Theorem Statement of a fact, idea, or rule. The fact is put through a process of reasoning that proves whether it is true or false.
Proof Step-by-step process used to find out whether a theorem is true or false.

Our example theorem is the formula for the area of a triangle:
( b × h ) / 2
We start with a right triangle, and then use that answer to prove the formula for any triangle.

Prove the Formula for a Right Triangle

A right triangle has one 90 degree angle. You can use a rectangle to prove the formula for the area:
( b × h ) / 2

  1. Draw a rectangle and label the points A, B, C, D. Insert a small square in the bottom left corner to indicate a right angle (90 degrees). Add labels for h, the height, and b, the base.

    The area of your rectangle is
    b × h

  2. Draw a line from A to C. This divides the rectangle into two equal right triangles.

    The area of each triangle is half the rectangle’s area. Therefore, the area for either triangle is
    ( b × h ) / 2

Prove the Formula for Any Triangle

The first proof only works for a right triangle. The second proof works for any triangle.

  1. Draw a rectangle that does not contain any right angle.

  2. Draw a line straight down from vertex B. Label the line h for height. Insert a small square at the bottom to indicate a right angle (90 degrees).

  3. Label the point at the bottom of the line E. Label the two line sements to the left and right x and y.

  4. You now have two RIGHT triangles, ABE and CBE. You can find their areas by using the formula for right triangles:
    ( b × h ) / 2
    The base (b) for ABE is y and the base (b) for CBE is x.

    The large triangle ABC is composed of the two smaller triangles ABE and CBE. Therefore, the area of ABC = the area of ABE + CBE.

    You can express this with any of the following equations:

    Area of triangle ABC = ( y × h ) / 2 + ( x × h ) / 2

    Area of triangle ABC = ( y × h + x × h ) / 2

    Area of triangle ABC = h × ( y + x ) / 2
