Tips for user documentation.

What makes good user documentation?

• Simplicity. Clarity. Complex topics are easy to understand.

• You can easily find the content you're looking for.

• The most important content comes first.

How do you make something easy to understand?

• Organization.

• Clear language.

• Willingness to be the user.

Tips for clear language.

Use active voice:

"The sun painted a golden path on the ocean."

Not passive voice:

"The golden path was painted on the ocean by the sun."

Simplify the action:

"The sailboat sailed smoothly for days."

Don't use convoluted tense:

"The sailboat had been smoothly sailing for days."

Use the second person:

"If you hold a seashell to your ear, you can hear the ocean!"

Don't be formal or cold:

"When a seashell is held to one's ear, the ocean can be heard."

Be concise:

"To see the oceanfront, ride your bicycle down the Promenade."

Don't write complicated content:

"People-centered yet concrete-like, the Promenade allows fully-geared duo-wheeled pedalers to wanderingly navigate amongst and beside the cutting-edge hydro-like global-oriented water-front solution."
